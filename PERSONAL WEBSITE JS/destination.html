Okinawa

Okinawa, Japan

  • Population: 141,570 (2017)
  • Latitude: 26°20' N
  • Longitude: 127°48' W

About:

Okinawa is a picturesque island located in the southernmost part of Japan, known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. As the largest of the Ryukyu Islands, Okinawa has a unique history influenced by its indigenous Ryukyuan culture and the legacy of World War II. I feel that is the other side in Japan that is not that popular and a way more authentic destination to learn about japanese culture.

New Orleans

Louisiana, USA

  • Population: 391,006 (2022)
  • Latitude: 29°57' N
  • Longitude: 90°4' W

About:

New Orleans is a city in Louisiana, known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and unique blend of French, African, and American influences. Famous for its Mardi Gras celebrations, jazz music, and Creole cuisine, New Orleans is a destination like no other. Landmarks include the French Quarter, Bourbon Street, and the Garden District. The city's spirit of resilience is palpable, particularly in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina. Chongqing

Chongqing, China

  • Population: 22,251,500 (2022)
  • Latitude: 29°33' N
  • Longitude: 106°33' W

About:

Chongqing is real life cyberpunk 2077, is a sprawling metropolis in southwestern China, known for its dramatic hilly landscape, which has earned it the nickname "Mountain City." As one of China's four direct-controlled municipalities, Chongqing is a major economic center and transportation hub in the region. The city is famous for its spicy Sichuan cuisine, particularly hotpot, and its rich cultural history that includes ancient architecture, temples, and the stunning Three Gorges along the Yangtze River. Chongqing's rapid urban development is juxtaposed with its lush natural scenery, making it a unique blend of modernity and tradition.

Bangkok

Central Thailand, Thailand

  • Population: 8,305,218 (2021)
  • Latitude: 13°45' N
  • Longitude: 100°29' W

About:

Bangkok is the vibrant capital city of Thailand, known for its bustling streets, ornate temples, and vibrant nightlife. As a cultural and economic hub, Bangkok seamlessly blends the old with the new, offering a mix of traditional Thai architecture, such as the Grand Palace and Wat Arun, alongside modern skyscrapers and shopping malls. The city is famous for its street food, markets like Chatuchak, and the Chao Phraya River, which provides a scenic backdrop to many of the city's attractions. Bangkok’s lively atmosphere and rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination in Southeast Asia. Berlin

Berlin, Germany

  • Population: 3,878,100 (2023)
  • Latitude: 52°31' N
  • Longitude: 13°24' E

About:

Berlin is the dynamic capital of Germany, known for its rich history, cutting-edge architecture, and vibrant arts scene. The city has been at the center of significant historical events, from the Prussian Empire to the fall of the Berlin Wall, and today it stands as a symbol of unity and progress. Berlin is home to iconic landmarks like the Brandenburg Gate, the Berlin Wall Memorial, and the Reichstag building. With its thriving cultural scene, diverse neighborhoods, and renowned nightlife, Berlin is a city that constantly reinvents itself while honoring its past.
